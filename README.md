\begin{abstract}
Prime numbers are redefined here not merely as indivisible integers but as \textbf{irreducible ground states} in the Tanfarid Quantum Thermodynamic Universe (TQTU). Through resonance scoring, gamma-stability perturbation, and orbital structural filters (OSF), primes are shown to maintain unique resonance signatures that composites cannot fully mimic. This theorem reframes prime theory as a dynamic law of universal resonance.
\end{abstract}

A prime number is not only defined by divisibility but by its \textbf{irreducible resonance ground state}.

Every prime up to 20,000 passes three invariants:
\begin{enumerate}
